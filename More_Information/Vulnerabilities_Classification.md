Within the domain of smart contract security, there is, to the best of our knowledge, no standardized approach for evaluating vulnerability risk. Different surveys and vulnerability detection tools assign varying levels of severity based on their own criteria. Hence, we decided to utilize our own classification for this study. To achieve that, first, we unified vulnerabilities of the same kind detected by different analyzers. Our mapping strategy can be found in the [groups file](../Reports/Vulnerability_groups.json). Subsequently, we classified the remaining vulnerabilities into categories of critical, high, medium, and low severity. Below, we present our classification. It is important to note that we retained the original vulnerability names provided by each analyzer tool.

| **Critical**   | - access_control<br>- Unchecked Calls<br>- front_running<br>- Locked Ether<br>- Reentrancy |
| **High**       | - A miner can use others input to gain a benefit himself<br>- denial_of_service<br>- Operation may lead to a denial of essential contract function<br>- Over/Underflow<br>- reentrancy-no-eth<br>- Visibility<br>- Low-Level Calls<br>- encode-packed-collision<br>- Weak Rand |
